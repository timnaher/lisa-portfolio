Riemannian Geometry · Neurobiology of Learning and Memory 2024

Riemannian Manifold Analysis of Infant Memory Formation

L. Bastian, E.-M. Kurz, T. Naher, K. Zinke, M. Friedrich & J. Born
SPD Manifolds pyRiemann Tangent Space EEG

Overview

Quantified convergence of neural voice representations after sleep in 3-month-olds. EEG mismatch responses were modeled as symmetric positive definite (SPD) covariance matrices on a Riemannian manifold, then projected to tangent space via Laplacian Eigenmaps. Sleep spindle activity predicted increased MMR similarity in late time windows.

Key Results

  • SPD covariance matrices from multi-channel frontal EEG, regularized and mapped onto the positive definite manifold
  • Tangent space projection via Laplacian Eigenmaps revealed cluster convergence of mother/novel voice representations after sleep
  • Local similarity via Euclidean distances in tangent space across early (220–382 ms) and late (686–918 ms) windows
  • MMR Similarity Index linked to frontal sleep spindle activity, connecting Riemannian distance metrics to memory consolidation

Method

Multi-channel EEG mismatch responses to the infant's own mother's voice and a novel voice were computed as SPD covariance matrices, capturing the spatial correlation structure of neural activity. These matrices live on the Riemannian manifold of positive definite matrices, where geodesic distances capture meaningful signal differences that Euclidean metrics would miss.

Laplacian Eigenmaps projected the high-dimensional SPD matrices into a low-dimensional tangent space, preserving local geometric structure. Distances between voice representations in this space served as a similarity index. The key finding: sleep spindle activity predicted post-sleep convergence of voice representations, providing a geometric account of memory consolidation in early infancy.
