fNIRS-Based Awareness Detection in Disorders of Consciousness

L. Bastian*, T. Näher* et al.
Neurophotonics 12(4) →
Clinical BCI Riemannian fNIRS MNE

Overview

Translated the Riemannian fNIRS framework into a clinical diagnostic tool for patients with disorders of consciousness (DoC). An individualized pipeline with session-specific hyperparameter tuning and permutation-based decisions achieved 100% sensitivity and 89% specificity for awareness detection.

Key Results

  • Individualized pipeline with per-participant covariance estimation, Riemannian classifier selection, and stratified cross-validation
  • Permutation-based clinical decision framework minimizing misdiagnosis rates in DoC
  • 100% sensitivity, 89% specificity for detecting awareness from fNIRS signals
  • Minimal setup burden — 2-second encoding, self-chosen motor/speech imagery, portable fNIRS cap for ICU bedside use

Method

Patients with disorders of consciousness are asked to perform mental imagery tasks (motor or speech imagery) while wearing a portable fNIRS cap at bedside. The pipeline extracts SPD covariance features from the hemodynamic response and applies individualized Riemannian classification to detect command-following ability.

Critical to clinical deployment: each participant's classifier is tuned independently with session-specific hyperparameters and validated via stratified cross-validation. A permutation testing framework converts classifier accuracy into a statistically grounded clinical decision, balancing sensitivity (detecting awareness) against specificity (avoiding false positives) to minimize the risk of misdiagnosis.
